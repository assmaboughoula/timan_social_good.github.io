Introduction

  • The TIMAN group is primarily composed of the graduate students, undergraduate students, and visitors working with Prof. ChengXiang Zhai. Since the group was founded in 2002, 17 Ph.D. students, 26 M.S. students, and 12 undergraduate students have graduated and landed on jobs in both industry and academia.
  • We work on a wide range of research areas, including information retrieval, text mining, natural language processing, machine learning, bioinformatics, and Web information management.
  • Our research has been supported by various government agencies (e.g., NSF, NIH, DHS, DOI, NASA, AFOSR, IARPA), companies (e.g., Microsoft, Google, Yahoo, IBM, HP), and private foundations (e.g., Alfred P. Sloan).
  • Group members have received many awards and honors, such as
    • Presidential Early Career Award for Scientists and Engineers (PECASE) (ChengXiang Zhai, 2004)
    • Sloan Research Fellowship Award (ChengXiang Zhai, 2008)
    • ACM Distinguished Scientist (ChengXiang Zhai, 2009)
    • IBM Faculty Award (ChengXiang Zhai, 2009)
    • ACM SIGIR 2004 Best Paper Award (Hui Fang, Tao Tao, ChengXiang Zhai)
    • ACM KDD 2006 Best Student Paper Award Runner-Up (Qiaozhu Mei, Dong Xin, Hong Cheng, Jiawei Han, ChengXiang Zhai)
    • ACM KDD 2007 Best Student Paper Award Runner-Up (Qiaozhu Mei, Xuehua Shen, ChengXiang Zhai)
    • Yahoo! PhD Student Fellowship (Qiaozhu Mei, 2007-2009; Xuanhui Wang, 2008-2009)
    • Google Ph.D. Fellowship (Maryam Karimzadehgan, 2010; Hongning Wang, 2012)
    • Yahoo! Key Scientific Challenges Award ( Maryam Karimzadehgan, 2009; Yuanhua Lv, 2010; Hongning Wang, 2012)
    • UIUC DCS C.L. and Jane Liu Award (Qiaozhu Mei, 2006-2007; Yue Lu, 2008-2009)
